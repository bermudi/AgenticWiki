# Eero Alvar

> YouTube creator exploring the frontier of [[agentic-engineering]]. Proposes [[software-factory|software factories]] — systems that map specs to finished software — as the next logical step beyond human-steered agent workflows. Focuses on the [[aiming-problem|aiming problem]] (tuning factories to produce quality output) and agent persistence (making agents immortal across context limits).

## Key Contributions

- **Software factory concept**: Framing automated software production as a mapping from spec-like inputs to finished software, with the structural problem of landing in the desirable subset vs. [[slop]]
- **Chaos analogy**: The mapping from specs to implementations is likely chaotic — small input changes produce wildly different outputs, making tuning difficult (Eero frames this as a "mathematically very unrigorous analogy" and assumes the answer is yes)
- **Agent persistence taxonomy**: Three approaches to extending agent life beyond context limits — new session tool, compact tool, and [[babysitter-agent|babysitter agent]] — presented as "first instincts or initial ideas," not established patterns
- **Babysitter agent**: An invisible agent managing another agent's context as its "subconscious mind" — the most novel of the persistence approaches
- **Tuning approaches**: Instruction libraries ("a library of instruction documents detailing every part of what goes into building software") and verification agents as aiming mechanisms
