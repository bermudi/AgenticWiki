# Key points extracted during ingest

- Martin Fowler and Kent Beck compare the AI revolution to previous software engineering shifts (OOP, the Internet, Agile).
- AI magnitude and speed of adoption are significantly faster than previous shifts.
- The "Snake Oil" industry: Every technological boom attracts opportunistic, low-quality solutions; AI is no exception.
- Agent Experience (AX) is Developer Experience (DX): Code that is easy for humans to understand (TDD, modularity) is exactly the type of code AI agents perform best with.
- The role of the programmer is shifting from writing code to understanding the domain and managing the "genie" (AI).
- Risk of "Re-soloing": AI may lead to programmers working in isolation again, losing the social benefits of pair programming and shared design.
- TDD is more relevant than ever as a verification mechanism for AI-generated code.
