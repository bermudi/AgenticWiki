# Karpathy on Claude Tag — "the third major redesign of LLM UI/UX"

> **Source provenance.** Andrej Karpathy's original X post (`x.com/karpathy/status/2069547676849557725`), posted ~23 June 2026 alongside Anthropic's Claude Tag launch. x.com blocks anonymous programmatic access (abuse-alleviation 403), so the text below is reconstructed **verbatim** from multiple independent secondary sources that quote the post in full — Latent.Space AINews ("Claude Tag: Multiplayer, Proactive, Persistent Agents in Slack," 2026-06-24) and quasa.io coverage — and cross-validated against the Theo (t3.gg) video transcript (`raw/yt-the-next-paradigm-shift-according-to-karpathy.md`), which reads both the opening post and the defense reply aloud verbatim. The wording is consistent across all sources.

## The original post

> This is a new paradigm for interacting with Claude that is significantly more "inline" with all the other human activity org-wide. Once you do all of the under the hood engineering work to make this just work, Claude basically joins the team in a seamless way. You can talk to it as you would talk to another person and it can help with a very large variety of workloads. In my opinion, this is the 3rd major redesign of LLM UI/UX. The first paradigm was that the LLM is a website you go to. The second was that it is an app you download to your computer. This third one is that it is a self-contained, persistent, asynchronous entity with org-wide tools and context, working alongside teams of humans. It really takes a while to wrap your head around it, but it works and it is awesome.

## The defense reply — "It's an org level harness"

After the post drew mockery on the timeline (Karpathy "got clowned on" for hyping what looked like a Slack bot — see the Theo video), he replied to someone defending him:

> I think a number of people on the timeline didn't read past the title and made inferences and comparisons that are just wrong and then used it as an opportunity to take cheap shots. This isn't a feature like some crappy Slack bot and it's certainly not a claw, though it has some aspects of it. It's an org level harness. The difference will become clearer over time.
